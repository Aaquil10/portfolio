Key Intelligence Discoveries

🕐
Peak Risk Hours
Crash frequency peaks during 5PM–7PM weekday rush hour. Alcohol-related severe crashes spike after midnight on weekends. Heatmap analysis revealed Friday evenings as highest combined risk.
🗺️
Spatial Concentration
Wards 2, 5, and 6 account for the majority of severe crashes. Central DC corridors show persistent hotspot patterns across all 15 years — risk is structural, not random.
🚶
Pedestrian Effect
Crashes involving pedestrians are dramatically more severe. When pedestrians are involved, injury/fatal outcomes dominate; property-damage-only rates drop sharply.
📊
Frequency ≠ Severity
High crash-frequency zones don't always overlap with high-severity zones. Some moderate-frequency intersections show disproportionately severe outcomes — missed by traditional frequency-only approaches.
🌧️
Weather Integration
NOAA weather data merged by date showed precipitation increases severe crash probability by ~18–23%. Reduced visibility conditions correlate with 31% higher fatal rates.
📷
Camera Effectiveness Varies
Truck restriction cameras showed the largest effect (−20%). Speed cameras showed minimal impact. Camera placement strategy matters more than camera quantity.
