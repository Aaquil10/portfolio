Published Research

Beyond Crash Counts: Modeling Traffic Crash Severity in Washington, DC, Using Machine Learning and Causal Inference

A large-scale, data-driven analysis using 300,000+ police-reported crash records across 15 years in Washington, DC. The study integrates geospatial hotspot analysis, a CatBoost gradient boosting classifier optimized for severe crash detection, and a Difference-in-Differences quasi-experimental framework to evaluate the impact of automated enforcement cameras. Findings show that severe crash risk is spatially concentrated — not random — and that targeted AI-powered enforcement can moderate crash growth in high-risk urban corridors.
